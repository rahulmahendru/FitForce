Protein

Why is Protein important?

Build and repair muscles and tendons
Essential for body growth
Formation of enzyme molecules
Antibody proteins help in protecting against diseases

How much protein should I take?

The amount of protein one should consume will depend on personal goals.

As a general rule, your minimum protein intake should be around 0.8 grams per your bodyweight in kilograms, which would change to 1.3 to 1.7 if you are trying to grow muscle through physical activity. You should aim to fill 1/4 of your plate with protein rich foods.

Personally, I like to aim for 25-30% of my calories to come from protein.

Myths about protein

  • Myth: Vegetarians and vegans do not get enough protein from their diet.

    Truth: There are many vegan and vegetarian food sources that contain protein and are enough to reach daily protein targets.

  • Myth: Protein from animal foods is better than vegetarian foods.

    Truth: The quality of protein depends on its chemical structure and not the source it comes from.

  • Myth: Protein supplements are essential if you are gym going person.

    Truth: Supplements should only be used if you are not able to fulfil your intake goals from food.

  • Myth: Protein supplements are bad for your body.

    Truth: Supplements from good companies are totally safe to use and have no side effects whatsoever.
