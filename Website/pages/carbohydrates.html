Carbohydrates

Why are Carbohydrates important?

Main Source of Energy in the body
Provide fuel for body to function
Enable Fat Metabolism
Essential for brain functioning, memory & mood

How much Carbohydrates should I take?

Around 35% - 55% of your calories should come from carbohydrates.

A good idea is to fill 1/4 of your plate with complex carbohydrates and 1/4th with simple carbohydrates.

What foods are rich in Carbohydrate?

We should aim to avoid bad carbs that come from high calorie food and refined food products. Rather, try to focus on complex carbs.

Myths about Carbohydrates

  • Myth: Carbohydrates make you gain weight

    Truth: Carbohydrates are essential nutrients that provide daily source of energy to the body.

  • Myth: Carbohydrates should not be eaten at night

    Truth: Carbohydrates are processed by our body the same way as other nutrients. Eating 'good' carbohydrates at night will not make you gain weight.
